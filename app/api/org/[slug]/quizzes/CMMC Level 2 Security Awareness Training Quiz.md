# CMMC Level 2 Security Awareness Training Quiz

This 20-question quiz is designed to assess understanding of the CMMC Level 2 Security Awareness Training content, covering controls AT.L2-3.2.1, AT.L2-3.2.2, and AT.L2-3.2.3.

---

### AT.L2-3.2.1: General Security Awareness

**1. What is the primary purpose of CMMC Level 2 certification?**

a) To protect company proprietary information.

b) To protect Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) and remain eligible for DoD contracts.

c) To achieve the highest possible level of cybersecurity maturity.

d) To train employees on basic IT skills.

**Answer: b**

**2. Which of the following is the best definition of Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI)?**

a) Any information that is not classified as Secret or Top Secret.

b) Information the government creates or that an entity creates for the government that requires safeguarding.

c) Company trade secrets and financial data.

d) All emails and documents created within the company.

**Answer: b**

**3. What is a key difference between CUI and "Company Proprietary" information?**

a) CUI is less sensitive than proprietary information.

b) There is no difference; the terms are interchangeable.

c) CUI is governed by federal law and mandatory NIST controls, while proprietary information is governed by company policy.

d) Only CUI needs to be marked; proprietary information does not.

**Answer: c**

**4. According to the training, which of the following is NOT one of the five non-negotiable rules for CUI handling?**

a) Store CUI on approved enclave systems only.

b) Verify need-to-know before accessing CUI.

c) Share CUI with any US-based company.

d) Use approved encrypted channels for transmitting CUI.

**Answer: c**

**5. You receive an email with the subject line "Project Alpha Technical Specifications." What is the correct way to mark this email if it contains CUI?**

a) No marking is needed if it is sent internally.

b) Forward the email to the security officer to mark it.

c) Add "[CUI]" to the beginning of the subject line.

d) Mark the body of the email, but not the subject.

**Answer: c**

**6. Which of the following is a primary red flag for a phishing email?**

a) The email is from a known contact.

b) The email contains a link to the company website.

c) The email uses urgent language like "Immediate Action Required" and has a suspicious sender domain.

d) The email has a well-designed company logo.

**Answer: c**

**7. What is the recommended best practice for creating a strong password for the CUI enclave?**

a) Use a short, complex password like `P@ssw0rd1`.

b) Use a long, memorable passphrase like "Correct-Horse-Battery-Staple".

c) Reuse your personal email password to make it easy to remember.

d) Write your password on a sticky note attached to your monitor.

**Answer: b**

**8. What should you do every time you step away from your workstation?**

a) Log off completely.

b) Ask a coworker to watch your screen.

c) Lock your screen using Win+L or Cmd+Ctrl+Q.

d) Turn off your monitor.

**Answer: c**

**9. If you suspect a security incident, what is the first step you should take?**

a) Try to fix the problem yourself.

b) Stop working, disconnect from the network if safe, and do not reboot.

c) Immediately email your manager with the details.

d) Delete any suspicious files or emails.

**Answer: b**

### AT.L2-3.2.2: Role-Based Training

**10. What is the "Golden Rule" for System Administrators with privileged accounts?**

a) Use the same password for both admin and regular user accounts.

b) Never browse the internet or check email while logged in with a privileged account.

c) Share admin credentials with other team members to save time.

d) Apply security patches only when they become critical.

**Answer: b**

**11. For a Developer, what is the correct way to handle API keys and other secrets?**

a) Hardcode them directly into the source code for easy access.

b) Store them in a text file in the same directory as the application.

c) Use environment variables or a dedicated secrets management vault.

d) Email the keys to the rest of the development team.

**Answer: c**

**12. What is a key security responsibility for a Manager or Supervisor?**

a) To personally fix all security issues for their team.

b) To approve all access requests without question to avoid delays.

c) To verify the "need-to-know" for access requests and conduct annual access recertification.

d) To install security software on their team members' computers.

**Answer: c**

**13. A member of the Finance team receives an email from the "CEO" requesting an urgent wire transfer to a new vendor. What is the most critical action to take?**

a) Process the transfer immediately as requested by the CEO.

b) Reply to the email to ask for more details.

c) Forward the email to the accounting department.

d) Verify the request with the CEO through a secondary channel, like a phone call, before taking any action.

**Answer: d**

**14. What is the primary security duty of a Contracts & Procurement professional when sharing CUI with a subcontractor?**

a) To get verbal confirmation that the subcontractor is secure.

b) To ensure the subcontractor has the appropriate CMMC clauses (e.g., DFARS 252.204-7012) in their agreement.

c) To send the CUI in a password-protected ZIP file.

d) To assume the subcontractor is compliant if they are a US-based company.

**Answer: b**

### AT.L2-3.2.3: Insider Threat Awareness

**15. Which of the following best describes a "Negligent Insider"?**

a) An employee who intentionally steals data for personal gain.

b) An employee whose credentials have been stolen by an external attacker.

c) An employee who accidentally exposes data or falls for a phishing scam.

d) A former employee who still has access to the network.

**Answer: c**

**16. Which of the following is considered a *behavioral* indicator of a potential insider threat?**

a) Large file transfers to a personal cloud storage account.

b) Repeated attempts to access restricted folders.

c) Sudden, unexplained disgruntlement and conflicts with coworkers.

d) Logins from a foreign IP address at 3 AM.

**Answer: c**

**17. A user on your team who has never worked on weekends suddenly starts working late and on Saturdays without a clear business reason. This could be an indicator of what?**

a) The employee is a very dedicated worker.

b) A potential insider threat, specifically the "Unusual Hours" indicator.

c) The employee is having trouble with their computer.

d) The employee is preparing for a promotion.

**Answer: b**

**18. Which of the following is considered a *technical* indicator of a potential insider threat?**

a) An employee complains frequently about their supervisor.

b) An employee is experiencing significant financial stress.

c) An employee is observed taking photos of their screen.

d) A large volume of data is transferred to a personal USB drive.

**Answer: d**

**19. What is the most critical aspect of the company's policy on reporting insider threat concerns?**

a) You must have conclusive proof before reporting someone.

b) Reports are handled with confidentiality, and there is a strict no-retaliation policy for good-faith reporting.

c) You should only report concerns to your direct manager.

d) All reports are made public to ensure transparency.

**Answer: b**

**20. According to the training, who is considered the "best sensor" for detecting behavioral changes that could indicate an insider threat?**

a) The IT department.

b) The employee's colleagues and direct manager.

c) The company's security cameras.

d) The Facility Security Officer (FSO).

**Answer: b**
